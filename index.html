About Me

I am a scientist at the Vikram Sarabhai Space Center, Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where I work on space mechanisms and robotics. My research interests lie at the intersection of optimal control theory, stochasticity, and machine learning. Specifically, I am driven by the research on modeling, trajectory planning, and optimal control of dynamical systems with uncertainties.

Before joining ISRO, I completed my bachelors in aerospace engineering from the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST). During my bachelors, I worked as a research intern at VSSC and IIST, where I had the opportunity to collaborate with Dr. Pankaj Priyadarshi, Dr. Raveendranath, and Dr. Aravind Vaidyanathan on my thesis-based project and internship. I worked on a novel vertical landing stage recovery approach for the launch vehicle, where I modeled the landing dynamics and optimized the re-entry trajectory. I later extended it and found the stochastic optimal touch-down state vector for safe recovery under uncertainties. I also worked on co-optimizing the launch vehicle design and the ascent trajectory in my internship (Check out my research projects).

Currently, I am working on 1) stochastic optimal control for the trajectory optimization problems and 2) smooth motion planning of the robotic manipulator for in-space activities.
