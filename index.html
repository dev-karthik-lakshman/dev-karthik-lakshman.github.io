About Me

I am a scientist at the Vikram Sarabhai Space Center, Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where I work on space mechanisms, robotics and other complex dynamical systems. My research interests lie at the intersection of optimal control theory, dynamical systems, data-driven modeling, and optimization under uncertainties. Specifically, I am driven by the research on

  • Stochastic optimal control
  • Optimization under uncertainty
  • Trajectory optimization
  • Real-time learning with sparse data
  • Coupled systems
  • Multi-disciplinary Design Optimization
  • Inverse Design

Before joining ISRO, I completed my bachelors in aerospace engineering from the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST). During my bachelors, I worked as a research intern at VSSC and IIST, where I had the opportunity to collaborate with Dr. Pankaj Priyadarshi, Dr. Raveendranath P, and Dr. Aravind Vaidyanathan on my thesis-based project and internship. During my project, I worked on a novel vertical landing stage recovery approach for the launch vehicle, where I modeled the landing dynamics and re-entry trajectory of the spent stage. Later, I coupled the systems using the Multi-disciplinary feasible (MDF) architecture and computed the optimal trajectory parameters and touchdown state vector. Further, I extended it and added the uncertainties into the problem. I formulated an inverse problem to compute the optimal touch-down state vector satisfying the safety chance constraints with desired probabilites. I also worked on Multi-disciplinay Desion Optimization (MDO) of Solid-Rocket Motor based launch vehicle with various coupled systems. (Check out my projects).

Currently, I am working on 1) stochastic optimal control for the trajectory optimization problems and 2) Optimal trajectory planning of the robotic manipulator for in-space activities.
